# Executive Summary

This project documents a forensic examination involving BitLocker-encrypted media and the acquisition of protected data within a controlled forensic environment.

The investigation focused on identifying encrypted volumes, recovering BitLocker key protector information, acquiring a logical forensic image of the encrypted drive, validating evidence integrity through cryptographic hashing, and documenting system artifacts relevant to forensic analysis.

Industry-standard forensic procedures were followed throughout the acquisition and validation process to ensure evidentiary integrity and repeatability. The resulting evidence was preserved and verified using FTK Imager, BitLocker management utilities, and cryptographic hash validation techniques.

The project demonstrates practical experience with Windows encryption technologies, forensic acquisition methodologies, evidence integrity verification, and encrypted data preservation procedures commonly encountered during digital forensic investigations.
